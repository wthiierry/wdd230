Hick's Law

Apple

https://www.apple.com/

This exemplifies Hick's Law in action. The website has been intricately crafted to guide visitors towards taking actions, such as purchasing the product or delving deeper into learning about it. The website exhibits a high level of sophistication in its design and functionality.

Visual Hierarchy

The New York Times

https://www.nytimes.com/

This serves as an illustration of Visual Hierarchy principles at work. The company's name takes center stage, standing out as the most prominent element, akin to hot breaking news. As you scroll down the page, additional news items become visible, yet they are presented in a relatively smaller size compared to the headline news at the top. Furthermore, the font size and image dimensions dynamically adjust in accordance with their perceived interest to readers.

Rule of Thirds

Amazon

https://www.amazon.com.br/

Amazon's homepage is a prime example of adhering to the Rule of Thirds. From the top to the middle of the page, they've neatly divided it into nine equally-sized sections, each housing text and content. In the middle section, there's a wide horizontal box for displaying rotating, smaller images. As you scroll down, they continue to follow this pattern with one row of three evenly spaced boxes followed by one row of the horizontal wide box. This consistent structure of three boxes, one horizontal box, and repeat, continues until you reach the end of their webpage.
